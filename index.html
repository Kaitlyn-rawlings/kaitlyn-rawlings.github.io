Background

About

I'm an environmental scientist and spatial analyst based in Kansas City, with a background spanning field ecology, cartography, and municipal GIS. My work sits at the intersection of ecological science and data infrastructure — using spatial tools to understand and restore the landscapes that water shapes.

As a GIS Technician with the City of Lenexa, I build workflows and analyses that support stormwater management, infrastructure planning, and public works. Before that, I worked in telecom GIS, managed international scientific conferences, and spent a year traveling solo through twelve countries.

I'm pursuing a funded thesis-based MS in restoration ecology or watershed science, with a focus on river restoration, Low-Tech Process-Based Restoration (LTPBR), and field-integrated GIS. I'm drawn to programs with strong advisor relationships and hands-on fieldwork in warm, dry climates.
